UNC Charlotte graduate programs continue climb in U.S. News Rankings

UNC Charlotte's graduate programs have seen significant advances in the 2025 U.S. News & World Report Best Graduate Schools rankings, reflecting its expanding national reputation as a top-tier center for teaching, research, and graduate education. This surge in rankings coincides with the university's recent attainment of the Carnegie R1 classification for research activity, placing it among the top 4% of U.S. research institutions.

Highlighting its achievements, the Charlotte MBA program holds its position as the No. 1 part-time MBA in North Carolina's public universities and ranks No. 49 nationally. The School of Nursing's master’s program notably rose 41 spots to No. 68, while its Nurse Anesthesia program climbed 18 positions to No. 33. Additionally, the Doctor of Nursing Practice program debuted impressively at No. 85 nationally.

The Cato College of Education's Special Education programs entered the rankings at No. 21, and the Public Affairs program ascended to No. 49 nationally, with three of its concentrations ranking in the national top 25. The Clinical Psychology program also improved, now ranked No. 78 nationally.

Chancellor Sharon L. Gaber and Associate Provost Bill Tolone emphasized the comprehensive quality and real-world impact of UNC Charlotte’s graduate programs, which now boast over 20 top-100 placements across various disciplines. With more than 6,200 graduate students and a record enrollment, UNC Charlotte is solidifying its stature as a leading public research institution.

Project A14 is a Single Page Application (SPA) developed to deliver the latest news from UNC Charlotte for the month. The application leverages a modern tech stack to ensure fast, responsive, and interactive user experiences. The backend is built with NodeJS, providing a scalable and efficient server-side solution. NodeJS's non-blocking I/O and event-driven architecture make it ideal for handling multiple requests simultaneously, which is crucial for a news delivery platform expecting high traffic.

For the frontend, the application utilizes Angular. This framework supports dynamic content updates essential for a news SPA, allowing for seamless content refreshes without reloading the entire page. Angular's robust data-binding capabilities ensure that the latest news is consistently presented to the user in real-time.

The data is managed using MongoDB, a NoSQL database known for its flexibility and scalability. MongoDB's schema-less nature allows for the storage of diverse news content in various formats, making it easy to update and retrieve data as the news evolves. This database choice complements the real-time, dynamic nature of the news application by facilitating rapid queries and updates.

Together, this tech stack provides a solid foundation for Project A14, supporting its goal to deliver timely and engaging news updates to the UNC Charlotte community. This infrastructure ensures that the application is not only robust and responsive but also capable of scaling to meet future demands as the user base grows.
